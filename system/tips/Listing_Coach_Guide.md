# Listing Coach — Trudy's step-by-step guide

Use **Listing Coach** when you add a **new item** and want the app to write the Etsy listing for you. You paste photos, enter basic purchase details, and the AI researches your item, writes the listing, and fills in all the fields. You review, adjust, and save.

**You do not need to write marketing copy or do internet research.** The AI handles identification, pricing research, and listing composition.

---

## Before you start

1. **AI must be set up** — Config → AI Provider (ask Scott if you see "AI not configured").
2. **Etsy connection is not required** to use Listing Coach for writing listings.
3. Have your item photos ready in the **Photos** app on your Mac.

---

## Phase 1 — Log the purchase

### Open Listing Coach

1. Open the app → **Inventory** tab.
2. Click **Add new listing with Listing Coach**.
3. Click **Start** on the welcome screen.

### Paste your item photos

1. In **Photos**, select the pictures for this item (click one, or Shift-click several).
2. Press **⌘C** (Copy).
3. In Listing Coach, click the big **Item photos** box.
4. Press **⌘V** (Paste).

You can paste more than once until you have all your shots. Drag thumbnails to reorder — **best front photo first**.

**Tip:** Include close-ups, backstamp, and any flaws. The AI uses every photo for identification and listing quality.

### Enter purchase details

Fill in the basics about what you bought:

- **What is this item?** — A brief description (e.g. "Homer Laughlin fiesta ware dinner plate, yellow")
- **Date purchased** — When you bought it
- **Purchase price** — What you paid
- **Condition** — Select from the dropdown
- **Store category** — Your internal category for this item
- **Condition notes** — Any flaws, repairs, or things to mention (optional)

### Size & weight (optional)

If you know the dimensions and weight, enter them here. This helps the AI write accurate descriptions and is needed for shipping:

- **Weight** — with unit (oz, lb, g, kg)
- **Dimensions** — Length, Width, Height with unit (in or cm)

### Where I bought this (optional)

Record where you got the item:

- **Vendor / source** — e.g. Goodwill, estate sale, eBay
- **Vendor shipping cost** — what you paid for shipping to receive it
- **Reference #** — receipt number, order number, etc.
- **Purchase notes** — any notes about the purchase

### Add your own research (optional)

If the AI struggles to identify your item, you can paste Google Visual Search screenshots or research text. Click **Add my own research** to expand this section.

### Start the AI

Click **Research and compose listing**. The AI will take 30-60 seconds to research your item and write the listing.

---

## Phase 2 — Review and edit your listing

After the AI finishes, you'll see a single form with **everything** filled in — both your internal inventory fields and the Etsy listing content. Every field is editable.

### What you'll see

The form is organized in sections:

1. **Identity & Financials** — Item number (you fill this in), description, identification, status, quantity, sale price (AI-recommended), purchase cost, shipping cost, dates, tags
2. **Condition** — Condition code and notes (AI-enhanced for Etsy best practices)
3. **Etsy Listing** — Title, description, tags, category, era, materials, is supply
4. **Weight & Dimensions** — Weight, length, width, height with units
5. **Listing Workshop** — Internal strategy fields (title strategy, product story, etc.)
6. **Where I Bought This** — Vendor name, shipping, reference number, notes
7. **Internal Notes** — Private notes (not sent to Etsy)
8. **Photos & Quality** — Photo classifications, quality score, citations

### Fix anything with AI

**Per-field Fix:** Next to most AI-generated fields, you'll see a small **Fix** button. Click it, type what should change (e.g. "add the gold trim detail"), and the AI updates just that field.

**Global Fix:** At the bottom of the form, there's a text box: "Tell the AI what to fix or improve." Type your feedback (e.g. "Make the description shorter" or "Add detail about the markings on the bottom") and click **Fix it**. The AI updates whichever fields are affected.

You can use Fix as many times as you want before saving.

### Save to inventory

1. Make sure **Item number** is filled in (required).
2. Click **Save to inventory**.
3. The app saves everything, uploads photos, and generates a listing video automatically.
4. You'll be taken to the Inventory page with your new item selected.

After save, you can **Approve** the draft and publish to Etsy when your shop is connected.
