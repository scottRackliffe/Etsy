# Listing Coach — Trudy’s step-by-step guide

Use **Listing Coach** when you add a **new item** and want the app to write the Etsy listing for you. You paste photos, optionally paste your Google search results, answer a few short questions, and review the finished listing.

**You do not need to write marketing copy.** The app proposes answers; you mostly tap **Yes** or fix a word or two.

---

## Before you start

1. **AI must be set up** — Config → AI Provider (ask Scott if you see “AI not configured”).
2. **Etsy connection is not required** to use Listing Coach for writing listings.
3. Have your item photos ready in the **Photos** app on your Mac.

---

## Step 1 — Open Listing Coach

1. Open the app → **Inventory** tab.
2. Click **Add new listing with Listing Coach**.

---

## Step 2 — Paste your item photos

1. In **Photos**, select the pictures for this item (click one, or Shift-click several).
2. Press **⌘C** (Copy).
3. In Listing Coach, click the big **Item photos** box.
4. Press **⌘V** (Paste).

You can paste more than once until you have all your shots. Drag thumbnails to put your **best front photo first**.

**Tip:** Include close-ups, backstamp, and any flaws if you have them.

---

## Step 3 — Google Visual Search (recommended)

This is what you already do with great results:

1. In **Photos**, use the same photo (or your best one).
2. Right-click → **Search with Google** (or use Google Lens the way you usually do).
3. Take a **screenshot** of the results (or copy if you can).
4. In Listing Coach, click the **Google results** box and **⌘V** to paste.

If Google didn’t help this time, click **Skip — I didn’t use Google**.

---

## Step 4 — Review what the app found

The app will tell you:

- Whether your photos look clear enough
- What it thinks the item is (maker, pattern, type)
- A **suggested list price** (especially when Google results helped)

Tap **Looks right** or fix the identification in one line if it’s wrong.

---

## Step 5 — Price

- **Use suggested price** if it looks reasonable.
- **I know the price** if you already know what you want (common for items you’ve sold before).
- **Skip for now** if you’re not sure — you can add price later in Inventory.

---

## Step 6 — Quick questions

You’ll see a few cards with **suggested answers** already filled in:

- What is it?
- What’s included?
- Condition / flaws?
- Who buys this?

Tap **Yes, use this** on each, or **Edit** only if something is wrong.

---

## Step 7 — Review your listing

You’ll see the **title**, **description**, and **tags** the app wrote.

Read once for accuracy (especially condition). Then:

- **Save to inventory** — enter your **item number** and save.
- Or go back if you want to change an answer.

After save, you can **Approve** the draft and publish to Etsy when your shop is connected.
