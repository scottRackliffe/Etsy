Trudy's Classic Treasures — Our Story

It started the way the best things do — by accident.

Trudy had always been a history lover. Not the dusty-textbook kind, but the kind who picks up a piece of carnival glass at a yard sale and wonders who set it on their table for Sunday dinner in 1952. The kind who runs her fingers along the pressed pattern of an aluminum serving tray and imagines the holiday gatherings it anchored — the deviled eggs, the finger sandwiches, the laughter.

For years, she shopped for the pure joy of it. Estate sales on Saturday mornings. Thrift stores tucked behind strip malls. Flea markets where the real treasures hide under folding tables. She had a gift — an eye that could spot a piece of Depression-era glassware across a crowded booth or recognize a mid-century serving set buried under a pile of mismatched flatware.

Her shelves filled up. Then her closets. Then the spare bedroom.

One evening, surrounded by a particularly beautiful haul — a set of fire-king jadeite bowls, a cut glass candy dish that caught the light like a chandelier, and an embossed aluminum party tray still in its original box — she realized something. These pieces deserved more than storage. They deserved someone who would love them as much as she did.

Trudy's Classic Treasures was born.

What started as a way to share her finds became something deeper. Every item in the shop has been hand-selected — not by algorithm or wholesale lot, but by Trudy herself, one piece at a time. She gravitates toward vintage dishware with character: the teacups with gold rims that feel like a small luxury, the serving platters that turn an ordinary Tuesday into an occasion. She has a soft spot for elegant glassware — carnival glass bowls that glow amber in the right light, cobalt blue vases that look like they belong in a painting, milk glass pieces with that unmistakable creamy warmth.

And then there are the aluminum trays — hammered, embossed, etched — the unsung workhorses of mid-century entertaining. Trudy believes they're criminally underappreciated.

You'll also find unique household decorating pieces in the shop: the kind of items that make a room feel collected rather than decorated. A vintage wall pocket. A ceramic planter with just the right patina. Things that tell a story without saying a word.

Every item is carefully cleaned, honestly described, and photographed to show exactly what you're getting — because Trudy believes that buying vintage should feel like a discovery, not a gamble.

No jewelry — at least, not yet. Trudy says she's "not ruling anything out," which is exactly what she said before the aluminum tray collection took over the dining room.

Trudy's Classic Treasures isn't just a shop. It's one woman's love letter to the things that were made to last — and the belief that beautiful objects from the past still have a place at the table.
