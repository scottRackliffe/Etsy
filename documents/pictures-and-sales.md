# Why pictures are critical for sales

Good photos are one of the most important factors in selling on Etsy. This short guide summarizes why, and how the AiCE app helps you get them right.

## Why pictures matter

- **Buyers can’t touch the item.** Photos are the main way they judge condition, quality, and authenticity. Clear, accurate photos build trust and reduce returns and disputes.
- **Etsy’s rules require it.** Etsy expects [original photos of the actual product](https://www.etsy.com/legal/policy/listing-image-requirements/253962679005) that show condition, quality, and quantity. Stock photos or misleading images can get listings removed.
- **Search and visibility.** Listings with strong, consistent photos tend to perform better in search and in buyer decisions. First image (your main photo) is especially important.
- **Vintage and antique.** For vintage/antique items, Etsy and buyers expect you to document condition honestly—including any flaws—with specific terms (patina, crazing, foxing) and **photos that show all sides, marks, and defects**. Our condition section (grade + notes + up to 5 condition pictures) is built for this.

## What to do in this app

- Use the **directory picker** to select the folder that contains your item’s photos. Check the **preview** of images from that folder so you know you chose the right directory before importing.
- For **main pictures (up to 10):** Use your best shots of the item; first picture = primary listing image.
- For **condition pictures (up to 5):** Use photos that show any blemish, wear, or defect so the condition grade (Mint/Near Mint through Fair/As-Is) is clearly substantiated.

## Operator photo checklist (before listing request)

- [ ] At least one main photo exists.
- [ ] Hero photo is clear, bright, and representative.
- [ ] Condition photos show defects clearly (if defects exist).
- [ ] No misleading angle/cropping that hides flaws.
- [ ] Photos match item description and condition notes.

If this checklist is not complete, do not request listing generation yet.
